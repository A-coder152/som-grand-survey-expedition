Here at Hotland you can see the wonders of the Southern Ocean! We speak Hotlandish (Temperlathic), and English. Hotland is home to many indiginous groups that have helped shape our land! We cannot thank them enough! Here are some historic sites that you could see on your trip!

⚠️ WARNING!: OUR TEMPERATURES REGURALLY GO UP TO 2*HL (100*HLS, 32°C)

The Crying Man (Dasses of Home)

The Crying Man, (Dasses of Home, or directly translated: Man Crying) depitcs a man from the Nosticallic, crying. The man depicted is Emotinallyictisck, a man belived to have been deeply depressed after the destruction of his village during the War of The South (Sown of Conflict)

He was instrumental in the creation of the winning of the war.

The Tomb of the Warm (Temper of Pasth, Miter of Tomth)

Direct Translation: Hot Death, Water Tomb

This tomb contains the heroic soldiers who bravly defended our land from the Trianglish, during the War of the South. Many of these soldiers used heat to there advantage, but lots of them were wounded in battle, and they couldn't move, burning to death in the sun.

Sandfalls (Fown of, Dunds of Miter)

Direct Translation: Sand-Water Falls

This water fall is connected to Cave Dunowrath, a cave once connecting Hotland to 78. The water fall, formed after a fatal land slide, destroying villages, and collapsing caves. Now you can only get in through, by jumping in the deep underground lake.
You will not be able to get back up. (Travel to 78)
